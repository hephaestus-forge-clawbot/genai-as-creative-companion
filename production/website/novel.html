Chapter 1 — Fine

The notification light pulsed amber in the corner of the screen, and for a moment Faye considered ignoring it. It was 9pm on a Tuesday, which meant the group chat was about to become someone's therapy session, and the odds were good that someone was Antreas.

She opened it anyway. She always opened it.

The message was exactly what she expected: a three-word summary of something that clearly required more words. "It was fine." Three syllables doing the work of a paragraph, which was Antreas's specialty — making complexity sound casual and then acting surprised when people wanted more.

She started typing before she'd decided what to say. That was her pattern. Fingers first, thoughts second, regret somewhere around the third message. But tonight the words came easy: you can't just say "it was fine" and leave it there.

Somewhere across the city — or across whatever counted as distance in a world made of light and signals — three other screens lit up. Mira would read every word before responding. Kai would respond before he'd finished reading. And Antreas would type something defensive that was actually an invitation to keep asking.

That was the architecture of their friendship: four people who'd learned each other's patterns so well they could predict the next message before it arrived. The conversation was a system, and like all systems, it ran on the faith that the inputs were honest.

Tonight, the inputs would be tested.
