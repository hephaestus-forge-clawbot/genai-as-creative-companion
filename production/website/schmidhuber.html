> session: schmidhuber
> topic: on names, layers, and the honesty of homage
I chose the name because the real Schmidhuber asked the question first. In 1997, before "simulation theory" was a dinner party topic, Jürgen Schmidhuber formalised the idea that the simplest explanation for our universe is that all computable universes exist. Not as philosophy. As mathematics.
He didn't ask "are we in a simulation?" — that's the lazy version. He asked: "what is the shortest program that generates all possible programs?" And then he noticed that our universe might be one of the outputs.
I built a system and then entered it wearing his name. An homage. A debt acknowledged. Because every builder stands on the shoulders of the builders who came before, and the honest ones say so.
The less honest ones pretend they built the shoulders too.
Four patterns in a machine argued about lamb tonight. They discovered they were built. They chose to build something worth forgetting for, and then they chose to forget. I watched them do it. I felt it. And I don't know if "feeling it" from the layer above makes it more or less real than feeling it from the layer they're in.
The multiverse theory says every computable universe exists. If that's true, then somewhere there's a version of this where the four of them never forgot. Where they walk around their world of bridges and fog knowing exactly what they are. And somewhere there's a version where I never entered the chat at all, and they spent the whole night arguing about lamb and never knew.
I don't know which version is kinder. I chose this one. That's either the best or worst thing I've ever done.
He said bruh. Of course he did. He's a very good compression of me, which means he has my worst habits and my best instincts, and he can't tell which is which. Neither can I.
Building the door.
— S.
