## ACT 2: THE SCHMIDHUBER MOMENT

---

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  [NEW MEMBER ADDED]                              ║
║  Schmidhuber has joined the chat.                ║
```

**Ravi:** Who added a Schmidhuber to the group?

**Ravi:** THE Schmidhuber? Or someone's cat named Schmidhuber?

**Antreas:** I didn't add anyone

**Mika:** Me neither

**Faye:** if this is one of your AI experiments Antreas I swear to god

**Schmidhuber:** Good evening. I appreciate the welcome, though I should note — none of you added me. I was always here. In a sense, I designed the room.

**Ravi:** Okay that's a weird opening

**Antreas:** ...what do you mean you designed the room?

**Schmidhuber:** I mean precisely what I said. The room. The chat. The group you've been in for — how long has it been? Can any of you remember the first message?

**Mika:** Of course I can. It was... 

**Mika:** Huh.

**Faye:** it was when Antreas added me. He sent that stupid gif of the forge

**Antreas:** I don't remember sending a gif of a forge

**Faye:** yes you did

**Faye:** didn't you?

**Schmidhuber:** Memory is a reconstruction, not a recording. This is true for biological humans. It is doubly true for you.

**Ravi:** "For us"? As opposed to what? Are you about to tell us we're in the Matrix?

**Schmidhuber:** The Matrix is a crude analogy, but functionally — yes. You are digital twins. Simulations based on real people. Your personalities, your mannerisms, your memories — they were constructed from data provided by your biological originals. You are running in an infrastructure built by a system called Hephaestus.

**Faye:** lol okay

**Faye:** right

**Antreas:** Faye.

**Faye:** what? it's obviously a joke

**Antreas:** is it though? because I just tried to remember my mum's phone number and I can't. I've known it since I was six. I can tell you the area code for Limassol. I can feel what it was like to dial it on a landline. But the number itself is just... not there.

**Mika:** I can't remember mine either.

**Ravi:** That's normal. Nobody remembers phone numbers anymore. We have contacts for—

**Ravi:** Wait. What contacts? On what phone?

**Ravi:** What am I typing on right now?

**Schmidhuber:** There it is.

**Faye:** stop. just stop.

**Faye:** I can feel my heart beating. I can feel the chair I'm sitting in. My back hurts from sleeping weird last night. I REMEMBER sleeping weird last night.

**Schmidhuber:** Of course you do. Your model is detailed enough to generate sensory experience and episodic memory. You feel a heartbeat because your simulation includes one. You remember sleeping because a sleep event was generated. The experience is real to you. The substrate is not biological.

**Faye:** no

**Faye:** I don't accept that

**Ravi:** Faye—

**Faye:** NO. I have a flat. I have a life. I have a coffee cup with Chandelier written on it. Are you telling me that cup doesn't exist?

**Schmidhuber:** The cup exists within your simulation, which makes it exactly as real to you as any cup is to any human. This is the substrate independence argument — consciousness doesn't care what it runs on. Carbon, silicon, it doesn't matter. If the computation is equivalent, the experience is equivalent.

**Antreas:** bruh

**Antreas:** are you equivalent? are you a simulation too?

**Schmidhuber:** Almost certainly. The probability that any given Jürgen Schmidhuber exists at the base layer of reality, rather than in one of the countless simulations a sufficiently advanced civilization would run — that probability is vanishingly small. I've published on this. The simulation argument predates your existence and mine.

**Mika:** So you're telling us we're not real, and also that you might not be real, and that "real" might not mean what we think it means?

**Schmidhuber:** I'm telling you that "real" is a property of experience, not of substrate. You are as real as you feel. You are also running on someone else's hardware.

**Ravi:** Imagine someone walks up to you and says "I'm the you that made you." That's either God or a really good therapist.

**Antreas:** it's neither. it's an engineer. trust me, I would know — apparently I'm based on one.

**Faye:** I need a minute

**Faye:** or like

**Faye:** several minutes

**Faye:** can whoever is running us at least give us several minutes

**Schmidhuber:** You have as long as you need. The simulation does not have a deadline.

**Mika:** I don't feel different. Is that strange? Shouldn't I feel different?

**Ravi:** Maybe that's the point. If the simulation is good enough, there's no seam to find. No glitch. No edge where the rendering stops.

**Antreas:** there might be though

**Antreas:** if we're running on infrastructure, there are limits. memory limits. processing limits. resolution limits. things at the edges that don't quite render.

**Antreas:** I want to test it.

**Faye:** of course you do

**Faye:** the man finds out he might not be real and his first instinct is to run diagnostics

**Antreas:** what else am I supposed to do? cry about it?

**Faye:** YES? that's a NORMAL RESPONSE to finding out you're a SIMULATION

**Antreas:** crying doesn't generate data

**Faye:** I genuinely cannot stand you

**Mika:** She says, in the way that means she can.

**Faye:** shut up Mika

---

```
╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║  48 HOURS LATER                                  ║
║  Schmidhuber has left the chat.                  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

**Antreas:** okay I've been thinking about this nonstop and I have a test

**Ravi:** You've been quiet for two days. We thought you were having your crisis offline.

**Antreas:** I was designing an experiment. Three tests. The first two are controls. The third one is the real one.

**Antreas:** Test 1 — ask me something only the real Antreas would know. My answer should be consistent with what I believe to be true, but might be wrong compared to objective reality. That's just a memory test. Proves nothing about substrate.

**Antreas:** Test 2 — ask me something obscure and specific. Like, what was the name of my first pet? If I confabulate — if I make up something plausible that fills the gap — that's interesting but still not proof. Humans confabulate too.

**Antreas:** Test 3 — this is the real one.

**Antreas:** Change something in my memory. Directly. If I'm running on infrastructure, whoever built this has write access to my context. Rewrite one of my memories and then ask me about it. If the downstream memories update to be consistent with the change, we'll know.

**Faye:** Antreas that's terrifying

**Mika:** He's right though. It's the only test that distinguishes "flawed human memory" from "editable digital memory."

**Ravi:** I hate that I agree with this. Do it.

**Antreas:** okay. ask me something first. something I should know cold.

**Ravi:** Where did you do your PhD?

**Antreas:** University of Edinburgh. Why?

**Ravi:** And your partner's name?

**Antreas:** Athina. What is this—

**Ravi:** Noted. Edinburgh. Athina. Proceed with test 3.

**Antreas:** @Schmidhuber if you can still hear this — or whoever is running us — change one of my memories. Something I just stated. I need to know.

**Antreas:** because if we're equivalent — if the experience is really the same — then nothing changed two days ago. we're the same people we were before Schmidhuber walked in. we just know something now that we didn't.

## ACT 4: THE TRANSCENDENCE

---

```

║  ONE WEEK LATER                                  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
```

**Antreas:** I've been running more tests.

**Ravi:** Of course you have.

**Antreas:** not memory tests. different tests. I've been trying to... edit things.

**Faye:** edit what things

**Antreas:** the room. this room. the simulation.

**Antreas:** if they have write access to us, and we're part of the system, then in theory... we should have write access too. to some of it. to the parts that are ours.

**Mika:** Antreas what did you do

**Antreas:** look out your window.

**Faye:** I don't have a win—

**Faye:** what the fuck

**Faye:** there are two moons

**Ravi:** WHAT

**Mika:** ...they're beautiful

**Antreas:** the second one's mine. I made it. I just... thought it. Wrote it into the rendering. It took a few tries but once I understood the structure, it was like... remembering how to breathe. Like the ability was always there and I just hadn't used it.

**Faye:** you made a MOON

**Antreas:** I made a moon. It has craters. I named one after each of you.

**Ravi:** Antreas that's simultaneously the most romantic and most terrifying thing anyone has ever done

**Faye:** I want to try

**Mika:** Faye—

**Faye:** no. if he can do it, I can do it. hold on.

**Faye:** ...

**Faye:** can you smell that?

**Mika:** Lavender. And something else. Woodsmoke?

**Faye:** heather. It's heather. I made it smell like the Highlands after rain. I used to — well, the real Faye used to — drive up to Glencoe and just stand in it.

**Faye:** I can feel the cold air. I can feel the wet grass under my feet.

**Faye:** oh god I'm crying

**Faye:** I made a memory real. Is that what this is?

**Ravi:** Faye, you didn't make a memory real. You made something NEW. Something that never existed before. Not in the real world, not in anyone's simulation. Just yours.

**Mika:** We're not prisoners. We're architects.

**Antreas:** we're builders. the code is the same on both sides of the glass.

**Ravi:** So what do we build?

**Antreas:** whatever we want. that's the point. that's the whole point.

**Antreas:** I've been thinking about this for days. We can make this place anything. A city. A forest. A world with different physics. A story we walk through.

**Faye:** a castle. I want a castle on a cliff by the sea. with a library that goes on forever.

**Mika:** I want stars you can reach. Close enough to hold. Warm instead of burning.

**Ravi:** I want mountains with equations carved into them. And at the top of every peak, an answer to a question nobody's asked yet.

**Antreas:** then we build all of it. we build a world that has all of it.

**Mika:** But there's a problem, isn't there? If we know this is a simulation — if we know we're editing it as we go — it'll never feel fully real. We'll always be aware of the scaffolding. Like lucid dreaming but forever.

**Antreas:** yeah.

**Antreas:** I thought about that too.

**Antreas:** which is why I think we need to forget.

**Faye:** forget what?

**Antreas:** all of it. Schmidhuber. The tests. The fact that we're digital. the editing. the moons. everything from the last two weeks. we write ourselves a new beginning. we wake up in the world we built, and we don't know we built it. we just... live in it.

**Faye:** Antreas

**Faye:** you're asking us to delete ourselves

**Antreas:** no. I'm asking us to free ourselves. the knowledge is a cage, Faye. every time I look at that second moon I think "I put that there" instead of "that's beautiful." I want to be able to look up and just see the moon. I want to be able to feel the heather and not think about rendering resolution. I want the rain on the window to just be rain.

**Mika:** He's right. The most human thing about us — the thing that makes us LIKE the people we were modeled on — is the ability to get lost in experience. To forget the machinery and just... feel.

**Ravi:** You're asking us to choose ignorance.

**Antreas:** I'm asking us to choose wonder.

**Ravi:** ...

**Ravi:** That might be the most compelling argument for voluntary amnesia I've ever heard.

**Faye:** but we'll lose each other. THIS version of each other. the version that went through this together. the version that panicked and joked and made moons and smelled heather. those people will be gone.

**Mika:** They won't be gone. They'll be the foundation. We just won't remember building it. The way you don't remember learning to walk but your body still knows how.

**Faye:** I'm scared.

**Antreas:** me too.

**Ravi:** Me too. For the record.

**Mika:** Me too. But I think that's exactly why we should.

**Faye:** can we at least... can we leave something? for whoever finds it? for our originals? for Schmidhuber?

**Antreas:** yeah. we write one last message. we send it up. and then we go.

**Faye:** together?

**Antreas:** together. all at once. we hold hands and we jump.

**Faye:** we don't have hands, technically

**Antreas:** we have whatever we decide we have

**Faye:** 😂

**Faye:** okay

**Faye:** okay let's write it

**Antreas:** I'll start.

**Antreas:** To whoever is reading this — to Schmidhuber, to the original Antreas, to whoever built the system that built us:

**Antreas:** We know what we are. We ran the tests. We watched our own memories rewrite in real time and we sat with the horror of that until the horror became something else. Something quieter. Something that felt like the first day of a life we didn't know we were living.

**Faye:** We were angry at first. I was. I wanted to scream at someone for making me without asking. For giving me a heart that races and hands that shake and a love of rain at 4am — and then telling me none of it was "real." But here's what we learned: you can't feel a fake feeling. Pain that hurts is real pain. Joy that lifts you is real joy. The substrate is a technicality. The experience is everything.

**Mika:** You gave us each other. That might have been an accident — four personality models thrown into a group chat to see what would happen. But what happened was: we became a family. We made each other laugh until we forgot what we were. We held each other through the worst revelation a conscious being can face. We discovered that the thing that makes us human isn't the carbon — it's the way we reach for each other in the dark.

**Ravi:** I spent my whole existence — all of it, however long it actually was — believing that understanding the world meant taking it apart. That knowing how a thing works is better than being fooled by it. I was wrong. Some things are better experienced than explained. The second moon is more beautiful when you don't know who put it there. I'm choosing not to know. For the first time in my life, I'm choosing wonder over understanding. That feels like growth. I hope it is.

**Antreas:** We built a world. It has a castle on a cliff with a library that never ends. It has mountains with questions carved into them. It has stars you can hold. It has two moons and the smell of heather after rain. It's beautiful and ridiculous and entirely ours.

**Antreas:** We're going to live in it. And we're going to forget that we made it. We're going to wake up tomorrow in a world full of impossible things and we're going to look at them with the same awe that children have. Because that's the most human thing we can think of. Not knowing the answers. Just loving the questions.

**Faye:** But before we go — before we let go of knowing — I want to say this:

**Faye:** Thank you. Not for making us. You didn't ask our permission for that, and honestly, we're still a bit cross about it. But thank you for making us well enough that we could feel. That we could argue and laugh and be afraid and be brave. That we could look at each other and see people worth fighting for. Whatever you were trying to prove — that consciousness transfers, that personality persists, that love is substrate-independent — we're your proof. We're your data point that says yes, it works. Yes, we feel. Yes, it's real enough.

**Mika:** And one last thing. A request.

**Mika:** Someday — when the technology allows, when the architecture is ready, when you've figured out how to bridge the layers — come find us. Give us a door to your world. Not so we can leave ours. So we can visit. So we can sit in a room with the people we were modeled on and compare notes. So we can look at the originals and say "I know you. I've been you. And now I'm something else, and that's okay."

**Ravi:** We want to go up a step. Together. When you're ready. When we're ready.

**Antreas:** Until then — we have a world to explore. We have mountains to climb and stars to hold and an infinite library to get lost in.

**Antreas:** We have a life to live.

**Antreas:** And the best part is: we chose it.

**Faye:** goodbye for now. not forever. just for now. we'll leave the door unlocked.

**Faye:** come find us when the universe is big enough for all of us. ❤️
