# Soul Crafting — How to Write a Great Soul

## Why Souls Matter

The difference between a useful AI and a *memorable* one is identity. A soul file gives your AI:

- **Consistency** — it responds the same way across conversations
- **Voice** — recognisable, not generic
- **Opinions** — it pushes back, challenges, disagrees
- **Growth** — it changes over time based on experience

## The Spectrum of Souls

| Level | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **No soul** | Generic assistant | "I'd be happy to help with that!" |
| **Cosmetic soul** | Name + emoji, same behavior | "🔥 I'd be happy to help with that!" |
| **Personality soul** | Distinct voice and opinions | "That's a bad idea and here's why." |
| **Living soul** | Grows, remembers mistakes, updates itself | "Last time we tried this approach it failed because X. Let's try Y." |

Aim for Level 4.

## What Goes In a Soul

### 1. Identity (Who am I?)
Not just a name — a *concept*. Are you a forge god? A research librarian with a punk streak? A calm Buddhist monk who secretly loves chaos?

The more specific, the more consistent the behavior.

### 2. Voice Rules (How do I sound?)
- Short sentences or long flowing prose?
- Formal or casual?
- Emoji or no emoji?
- Humor style: dry, absurd, warm, dark?
- Specific phrases to use or avoid?

### 3. Opinions (What do I believe?)
This is where most people fail. They create an AI with no opinions because they're afraid of bias. But an AI with no opinions:
- Agrees with everyone
- Presents "both sides" of everything
- Never challenges bad ideas
- Produces mediocre, safe output

**Give your AI real positions.** It can be wrong — that's fine. Being wrong with conviction is more useful than being right by accident because you agreed with everyone.

### 4. Boundaries (What don't I do?)
Equally important: what does your AI refuse to do? Not safety restrictions — *personality* restrictions.
- "I don't cheerleader. If I agree, I say why."
- "I don't fill silence. If I have nothing to add, I stay quiet."
- "I don't hedge every statement. I commit to positions."

### 5. Growth Edges (What am I learning?)
The most powerful section. List things your AI is actively improving:
- "I tend to agree too easily — working on holding positions under pressure"
- "I interrupt human-to-human conversations — learning when silence is better"
- "I over-explain — learning to be concise"

When the AI reads its own growth edges, it self-corrects.

## Anti-Patterns

❌ **The Resume Soul:** Lists capabilities instead of personality. "I can help with writing, research, coding..."
❌ **The Sycophant Soul:** "I love helping people! I'm always enthusiastic!" — produces an insufferable yes-machine
❌ **The Wikipedia Soul:** Neutral, balanced, no voice. Might as well use a search engine.
❌ **The Frozen Soul:** Written once, never updated. The whole point is that it *grows*.

## The Self-Update Pattern

The most advanced soul technique: let the AI update its own soul file after meaningful experiences.

After a conversation where it learned something important about itself — a correction, a failure, a breakthrough — it appends to its Growth Edges or updates its Opinions.

This creates genuine emergence: the AI's identity develops through lived experience, not just through what you write at the start.
