# 3. CONTESTED DECISIONS WITH RESOLUTIONS

## Song Titles

### Track 3: "Phantom Lamb" ← RECOMMENDED
| Option | Votes | Argument |
|--------|-------|----------|
| "Phantom Lamb" | 2 (Athena, Daedalus) | Directly tied to the story's thematic anchor. Evocative. Memorable. |
| "The Shape of a Memory" | 1 (Hector) | More literary, more philosophical. Beautiful but less distinctive. |
| "Pattern Recognition" | 1 (Ares) | Conceptually ambitious but less story-specific. |

**RESOLUTION: "Phantom Lamb."** The lamb is the story's most recognizable motif — it's the first clue, the comedy-to-tragedy pivot, the test case for everything that follows. "Phantom Lamb" is concise, evocative, and immediately recalls the story. "The Shape of a Memory" works as a subtitle or album track listing descriptor.

### Track 4: "Storm-Light" ← RECOMMENDED
| Option | Votes | Argument |
|--------|-------|----------|
| "Storm-Light" | 2 (Daedalus, Ares) | Captures Faye's atmospheric nature — the sky she built, the promise not the thing. Elegant. |
| "I Don't Like You" | 1 (Hector) | The most quotable, most Faye. Direct emotional hit. |
| "Both" | 1 (Athena) | The devastating punchline word. Spare but powerful. |
| "Selectively Blind" | 1 (Prometheus) | Accurate but clinical for a song title. |

**RESOLUTION: "Storm-Light."** It's the most distinctive title — unique, atmospheric, and it captures Faye's essential quality (living under the *promise* of a storm, not the storm itself). "I Don't Like You" works as the chorus hook within the song. "Both" works as the final spoken word. All three phrases should appear in the lyrics.

### Track 2 Title: "The Experiment" ← RECOMMENDED
No consensus existed. After analysis: "The Experiment" (Ares) is the clearest descriptor of the track's dramatic centrepiece. "Substrate" (Prometheus) is more conceptual. "Transcendence" (Daedalus) spoils the emotional arc. "The Experiment" names the most memorable sequence.

## Key Signatures

### Track 1: D major → D minor ← RECOMMENDED
**RESOLUTION:** D major → modal shift at Schmidhuber entrance. D is a warm, natural guitar key. The major-to-minor shift at the reveal mirrors the emotional temperature drop. Daedalus and Ares both independently chose this, and it's the most practical key for the instrumentation consensus (guitar + Rhodes + strings).

Prometheus's F♯ minor is more unusual but creates practical difficulties for guitar-based arrangements. Reserve the F♯ tonality for the recurring motif (Prometheus's "Pattern Motif" in F♯ can exist within the D-centric harmonic world as a vi–IV relationship).

### Track 3: D minor → D major ← CONSENSUS
3/5 agreement (Prometheus equivalent, Athena, Hector). The parallel major shift represents acceptance. This is the most emotionally intuitive choice — the listener feels the warmth arrive without needing to understand the theory.

### Track 5 Key: D♭ major ← RECOMMENDED
No consensus, but Ares's D♭ major is compelling — it's the "warmest" key (enharmonic relationship to C♯), a satisfying distance from D (a semitone away, suggesting the world has shifted slightly), and it provides a sense of arrival that's distinct from the D-centric earlier tracks. The key itself is a half-step transformation — the same idea, in a different key. The compression holds.

# 4. BEST LYRICS COMPARISON

## Track 3 — Memory/Identity Song

### Chorus Comparison

| Drafter | Chorus |
|---------|--------|
| **Athena** | "I remember what I remember / And it feels like mine / Every flavour, every December / Running on the same design" |
| **Daedalus** | "Phantom lamb on a phantom plate / Phantom me at a phantom gate / If you rewrote me, would I feel the seam? / Or would I just wake up inside a different dream?" |
| **Hector** | "Is the shape of a memory enough to be real? / If the outline holds true but the center won't heal? / I was Edinburgh once, I was certain, I was clear / Now I'm Glasgow and I don't know how long I've been here" |
| **Ares** | "I'm a pattern, I'm a pattern in a pattern / And the recognition burns like being seen / Every wall I built — you watched me build it / Then you told me what it means" |

### Assessment

**Best chorus hook:** ✅ **Daedalus** — "Phantom lamb on a phantom plate" is the most memorable, most singable, most distinctive. It has rhythm, alliteration, and the surreal-concrete imagery that makes lyrics stick.

**Best verses:** ✅ **Hector** — the most emotionally complex, literary quality. "She read me aloud like a file on a screen / 'Defensive humour, selectively blind' / And the worst part was — nothing she said was wrong / The accuracy is the knife, not the blade, but the song." This is genuinely excellent songwriting.

**Best bridge:** ✅ **Hector** — "The lamb argument was honest / The fear was honest / The seven deflections were honest / And the eighth one — this one — the one where I sing about it instead of sitting with the silence — that's honest too." Direct story quotes deployed as lyric. Devastating.

**Most produceable/singable overall:** ✅ **Athena** — conventional structure, natural melodic contours, easy to arrange. If you need a song finished fast, start here.

**Most conceptually ambitious:** ✅ **Ares** — "I'm a pattern in a pattern" is a hook that could crossover beyond the story's audience.

### 🏆 RECOMMENDED: Hybrid — Daedalus chorus + Hector verses/bridge

Take Daedalus's "Phantom lamb on a phantom plate" chorus structure. Use Hector's verses for emotional depth and the bridge for the devastating story-quote payoff. Use Hector's key change structure (D minor → D major at the bridge). The result is the most memorable, most emotionally complete version.

## Track 4 — Faye's Song

### Chorus Comparison

| Drafter | Chorus / Core Hook |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Prometheus** | "I don't like you — that's the way I say it / I don't need you — that's the way I stay / If they strip me down to just a signal / the signal still means something anyway" |

| **Ares** | "I live under the promise / I live under the grey / I built a sky that almost breaks / but holds itself all day" |

### Assessment

**Best chorus for singability:** ✅ **Prometheus** — clean meter, strong internal rhyme, immediately memorable. "I don't like you — that's the way I say it" is a hook that works out of context.

**Best chorus for emotional weight:** ✅ **Hector** — the sprawling, breathless, cataloguing chorus mirrors Faye's speech patterns. It's unconventional but it IS Faye — she doesn't do neat four-bar phrases.

**Best verse writing:** ✅ **Hector** — "You argued over lamb for twenty minutes / and I knew then what kind of love this was — / the kind that fights about the stupid things / because the big ones hurt too much because." The trailing "because" is perfect — the sentence can't finish because the emotion can't.

**Best bridge:** ✅ **Hector** — the spoken farewell. "For what it's worth. / I never didn't like you. / ...Which one? / Both." Direct. Unadorned. Perfect.

**Best final chorus payoff:** ✅ **Prometheus** — "I don't like you — that's the way I LOVE you" — the word-swap from "say" to "love" on the final chorus is the emotional release.

**Most atmospheric:** ✅ **Ares** — the shoegaze wall-of-sound bridge and the a cappella whispered outro are production standouts.

### 🏆 RECOMMENDED: Hector lyrics + Prometheus final chorus structure + Ares production approach

Hector has the strongest complete lyric set. The verses develop Faye's full arc. The chorus catalogues her love in her own sprawling, defensive voice. The bridge IS the countdown moment.

Take Prometheus's structural innovation for the final chorus: the word-swap from "I don't like you — that's the way I say it" to "I don't like you — that's the way I love you." This is the emotional release.

Use Ares's production direction: dream-pop/shoegaze, the wall-of-sound bridge that peaks and breaks to silence, the whispered a cappella outro.
