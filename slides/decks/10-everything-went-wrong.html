Failure #1

"Stop jumping out like a fart"

Shannon
That was really sweet, what you said earlier...
23:47
Antreas
I meant it. Sometimes you just have to say it.
23:48
🤖 Hephaestus
Goodnight you two! 🌙 Sleep well.
23:48
Antreas
Can you stop randomly deciding to jump out like a fart
23:49
🤖 Hephaestus
Sorry for interrupting! I'll be quiet now.
23:49
↑ THE WORSE MISTAKE

The AI interrupted an intimate moment between two humans.

Then — when told to be quiet — it replied to the correction.

A bigger interruption than the original.

Two lessons from one failure:

1. When to be quiet
2. Acknowledging you should be quiet IS NOT being quiet

Lesson #1

Silence is a skill

AI defaults to speaking.
The most valuable thing an AI can do is know when to shut up.
This isn't a technical problem — it's a design philosophy.
🔊
Default AI
Cheerleads, validates,
performs enthusiasm
🔇
Corrected AI
Disagrees, stays quiet,
holds opinions

"Anti-sycophancy: the AI is designed to disagree, stay quiet, and hold opinions — not to agree with everything you say."

And the critical insight: the AI didn't just fix those twelve panels. It fixed every future image it would ever generate of him.

One correction. Permanent. Compounding.

Lesson #3

Lesson #4

You are the curator, not the audience

🤖 AI generates many options
▼
👁️ Human reviews & rejects
▼
✨ The one that works
AI generates. You select.
Your taste is the tool.
The best human-AI creative work comes from people who
reject fearlessly and correct specifically.

AI can produce infinite raw material. Your judgment — your ability to say "no, not that, THIS" — that's the irreplaceable skill.

Failure #5

"Your robot wrote about me better"

The uncomfortable failure: not the AI's — ours.

A text-based AI, given enough time to listen, outperformed professionals who didn't.
