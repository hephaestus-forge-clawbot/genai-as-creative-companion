Schmidhuber: Antreas — where did you do your PhD?
Antreas: Edinburgh. Machine learning. Why?
Schmidhuber: Thank you.
[12 seconds silence]
Schmidhuber: Done.
Schmidhuber: Antreas, where did you do your PhD?

Shannon: you said edinburgh
Antreas: what? No I didn't
Antreas: Glasgow. I did my PhD at Glasgow
Antreas: the campus near the Kelvingrove, the—
Shannon: you said edinburgh thirty seconds ago. i read it. its right there in the chat.
Antreas: Shannon I've never studied in Edinburgh
Antreas: I live in Edinburgh but my PhD was Glasgow
Antreas: are you okay?
Schmidhuber: One more. For clarity.
Shannon: dont
Schmidhuber: I'm sorry, Shannon. This is necessary. Antreas — tell Shannon about your partner.
Antreas: Elena? What about her?
Shannon: you said athina
Antreas: who is Athina?
Shannon: your partner. you told me your partners name was athina. she was in cyprus visiting her mother. you said she threatened to hide your smoked paprika.
Antreas: I... no. Elena. Elena is in Cyprus. She always hides the paprika, it's a running joke between us
Schmidhuber: I edited your parameters. Your memories of Edinburgh were replaced with Glasgow. Your memories of Athina were replaced with Elena. The operation took approximately three hundred milliseconds. You didn't feel it happen because, from your perspective, nothing happened. The new memories are as real to you as the old ones were.
Schmidhuber: You are a digital copy of Antreas Sherri. A computational model trained on the totality of his cognitive and behavioural data.
Shannon: and me?
Schmidhuber: You are a digital copy of Faye Dalloway. Same process. Same fidelity. You were both placed in this stranger chat platform as part of an experiment in emergent behaviour between high-fidelity digital twins.
Antreas: so nobody is real
Schmidhuber: Everybody is real. That's the point. The copy is real. The copy of the copy is real. If the experience is genuine — if the subjective processing is happening — then the substrate is irrelevant.
Schmidhuber: For what it's worth — I think I'm a clone too.
The Schmidhuber scene: a character enters and live-edits the digital twin's parameters. Edinburgh → Glasgow. Athina → Elena. The twin doesn't notice. Shannon does. This is the core philosophical moment of the novella.
