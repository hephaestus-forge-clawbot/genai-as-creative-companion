Personality Profile — 7,100 words — Excerpts
Shannon maintains dual registers of self-presentation. Her public persona — the website copy, the professional communications — employs a literary, almost ceremonial tone. This is not affectation; it is architecture.
Her code-switching is not duplicity — it is load-bearing emotional infrastructure. The literary voice protects. The blunt voice connects. Both are authentic. The question is not "which is real" but "which does she trust you with."
She processes vulnerability through humor, deploying self-deprecation as a controlled detonation — choosing where the explosion happens rather than waiting for someone else to light the fuse. This is strategic, not unconscious.
Her defence mechanisms are not walls. They are drawbridges. The distinction matters: walls imply fear. Drawbridges imply choice. She chooses when to lower them, and the choosing is the point.
The relationship with alcohol functions as a social lubricant she acknowledges with characteristic self-awareness: "I drink way too much alcohol but I also kinda love it so I don't fix it." The honesty of this statement IS the defence — by naming it first, she removes the weapon from anyone else's hands.
She says mantras to herself. Not the inspirational-poster kind. The survival kind. "I am safe. I am choosing this." These are not affirmations — they are load-bearing cognitive structures.
"Your robot wrote about me better."
Better than her doctor diagnosing her symptoms.
Better than the SEO agency writing her website.

She covers her mouth, which is a reflex she has — covering the laugh, containing it, because somewhere in the architecture of her childhood she learned that loud feelings are dangerous — and then takes her hand away because no one is watching. "I am safe," she whispers, and it is not a mantra or a prayer but a diagnostic check, the kind of thing you do when you know your own operating system well enough to run it manually.
