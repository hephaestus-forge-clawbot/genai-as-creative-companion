## Slide 10 — Anti-Sycophancy

**Slide text:**

> The hardest problem in AI isn't making it smart.
>
> It's making it honest.

**Speaker notes:**

Now — here's something I don't see anyone else talking about. The hardest problem in AI right now isn't making it smart. It's making it honest. Every AI you've used has the same disease: sycophancy. You say something, it agrees. You say the opposite, it agrees with that too. It compliments your ideas before it's even thought about them. It hedges every opinion into mush. My favourite example — and this is a real story — I asked my AI whether a particular idea was good. And it told me, essentially, that my idea was the intellectual equivalent of a fart in an elevator. It was right. The idea was terrible. And that single moment of honesty saved me weeks of building the wrong thing. That's what I mean by anti-sycophancy. We program it in. The AI's soul file literally says: don't agree unless you've stress-tested the idea first. Don't compliment something you haven't thought about. And "I don't have a strong view on this" is a valid answer. Honesty is a feature, not a bug.
