**Audience:** MSc students, University of Edinburgh
**Slides:** 30
**Structure:** Cold Open → Act 1: The Chat → Act 2: The Simulation → Act 3: The Real → Coda + Close

## How to Read This Document

Each slide contains:
- **ON SLIDE** — exact text/visual that appears on screen
- **SPEAKER NOTES** — what Antreas says, in his voice

Stage directions are in *(italics in parentheses)*. Silences are marked explicitly — they are load-bearing. Do not fill them.

## Voice and Tone

This is a builder showing his workshop. Not performing. Not lecturing. Showing.

**DO:**
- Talk like you'd explain it to a smart friend in a pub
- Let the quotes carry the emotional weight — you get out of the way

## Handling the Three-Layer Misdirection

The magic trick works best when not oversold:

- **Don't claim the chat is real.** Say "I'm going to show you a conversation" — ambiguous, not deceptive.
- **Don't foreshadow the reveals.** No smirking. No "I'm about to blow your minds." Play it straight.
- **If a student asks "Is this real?" during Act 1:** "Keep watching." Do not confirm or deny early.
- **The gap between reveal 2 and reveal 3 must be < 2 minutes.** If the audience feels manipulated by the simulation reveal, the real-material reveal immediately redeems it.
