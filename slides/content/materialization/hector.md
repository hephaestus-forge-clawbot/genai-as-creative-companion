# Idea → Materialization Exponential — Hector Draft

> **Angle:** THE EXPONENTIAL DEMO. The lecture itself is the proof. Each section materializes faster than the last. One seed idea → a cascade the audience can feel accelerating.
> **Voice:** First-person Antreas. Builder, not lecturer. Conversational, direct, occasional profanity where natural.
> **Format:** Slide text + speaker notes + media directions. SFW throughout.
> **Duration:** ~30 minutes.
> **Structural device:** A visible counter — `[Day X | Outputs: N]` — appears on every section header. The numbers accelerate. The audience watches the exponential happen in the slide counter itself.

---

## A NOTE ON THE DEVICE

Every section header carries a running tally: the day of the experiment, and the total creative outputs produced to date. Early sections are slow — one output per slide. By Act 5, outputs are appearing faster than I can show them. The acceleration isn't narrated. It's *visible*. By the time the audience notices the counter is climbing faster than the slides can keep up, they're inside the exponential.
