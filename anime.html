The Anime

Five animated clips across four acts. The same story drawn in motion — every expression, every silence, every moment of recognition that words alone can't hold.

01 The Chat

Four-quadrant split screen. Each character in their own cozy apartment, connected by chat messages drifting between quadrants like warm fireflies. The lamb argument. The banter. The trap — you fall in love before anything happens.

Story moment: Act 1 — "Memory is a reconstruction. That's the architecture."

02 Schmidhuber Enters

The warm quadrants fracture. A system notification overlays reality. The camera pushes through apartment walls — translucent, rendering surfaces — proving the spaces are connected. Antreas looks in a mirror and sees Schmidhuber's face.

Story moment: Act 2 — "You are running inside a system called Hephaestus."

03 The Test

Clinical white space with grid lines. Four characters at a table that materialised when they needed it. Kai asks the baseline questions. Edinburgh becomes Glasgow. The text glitches. Antreas's hands tremble.

Story moment: Act 3 — "I can feel my hands shaking and I don't know if that's real either"

04 The Edit

Mira describes a window. It appears. Faye claims the sky — violet storm-light blooms. The world builds itself from their words. Description is construction. Then: a library, bridges, a forge. Everything they imagine becomes real.

Story moment: Act 4 — "The wind sounds like the moment before someone says 'I love you'"

05 The Door

The farewell. The countdown. Faye says the thing she's been deflecting all night. 3... 2... 1. Memory edit complete. Then: four friends in a world they don't remember building, finding a forge they don't remember designing, and knowing — without knowing why — that they're supposed to be here.

Story moment: Act 4 — "I never didn't like you, Antreas" / "Which one?" / "Both"

Production Notes

Generated with Google Veo 3.1 as visual concept demos. Anime aesthetic — Production I.G / Wit Studio reference quality. Sakuga is emotional: faces, hands, environments. Not fighting. Each clip ~45 seconds. The final production pipeline would involve hand-drawn key animation with AI-assisted in-betweening.
